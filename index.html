Original · Industry POV

I don't just run the pipeline — I notice where the content is going.

PROPEL didn't start as a business plan. It started with friends in the emerging AI-film and motion industry hitting the same wall: no trusted way to co-create and get credit for the work. So I built a co-creation network where creators collaborate under NDA/NCA protection from the first message, and an immutable ContribLog records who made what. I built it because I saw the problem — not for Prime, and not as a startup I'm trying to grow.

I'm sharing it because I think Prime Video would find it interesting — it's proof I read where a medium is heading, not just how today's assets move through a publishing workflow. But I'm not looking to run my own thing. I want to do work like this inside a powerful team creating real impact at scale — contributing as an operator who helps invent the next format, not just ship the current one.

Dow Jones event websites — recurring launches on fixed dates

▶ Full case study

As UX Program Manager on the Brand team at Dow Jones, Warren manages and oversees design updates across 10+ event & conference websites and their brand assets — for example JournalHouse and the WSJ Leadership Institute — for the company's brands across WSJ.com, Barron's.com, and MarketWatch.com. The work includes motion design and some video production, and supports ~8 events and 5 launches over the past five months. Each launch is a recurring, fixed-date go-live: exactly the deal-to-broadcast cadence, on repeat.

He leads a design system unifying those event-site builds into one — 6 of 8 core properties already migrated — cutting build time by ~80% and eliminating cross-property inconsistencies. He also built a robust AI tool for the intake process, receiving and triaging requests from six teams (subscription strategy, product design, product management, performance marketing, CRM, and event marketing), and owns the brand marketing team's Asana implementation for reporting, capacity, and resource management.

This is the strongest, most current proof in the catalog: recurring fixed-date launches, motion and video work, and one unified publishing system that makes every launch after the first run on rails.

▶ Full case study
