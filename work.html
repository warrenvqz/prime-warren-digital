tv.google & android.com — launch operations at scale

Director-rated Program Management Localization & Deployment New York / Remote
Play the playbook X-Ray my fit

This is the closest analog in Warren's catalog to operating a Prime Video surface. He managed updates for tv.google (Google TV) — a TV and streaming surface — alongside android.com (including the Android 12 launch) and wearos.google.com: a slate of marketing launches that go public on fixed, time-sensitive dates, across many surfaces, in many languages, with senior stakeholders watching. Warren ran it from inside an embedded pod at Huge, plugged directly into Google's marketing org.

He led design & dev roadmap planning and oversaw creative, content, site deployment, and localization across all three properties — with the Google TV surface and the Android 12 launch both landing on non-negotiable dates. He continuously adjusted priorities, plans, and resources to hit those dates. The mandate was simple and unforgiving: hit the dates without burning the team out.

The engagement actually ran on two fronts: this web-properties program, and a parallel year-long "Future of Google Search Ads" research & design program (its own title below). He maintained strong working relationships with Google's product marketing managers, strategists, and designers — translating engineering detail into business goals and back again. That is the operational shape of the Sports Ops role: a signed scope, a fixed air date, a cross-functional pod, localization and deployment pipelines, and executives who need calm, accurate readouts. The framing is Prime Video; the work is real.

Original · Industry POV

Reading where entertainment is heading — not just shipping today's slate.

Prime Video's mandate is to define the next generation of how customers watch. That is exactly why Warren built PROPEL — a co-creation network for the emerging AI-film industry, where creators team up under NDA-first protection from the first message and an immutable ContribLog records who made what. It exists because he watched a new class of entertainment form and saw the trust-and-attribution layer no one had built yet.

For a live-events and content operator, that is the tell: he doesn't just move today's assets through a publishing workflow — he reads where the medium is heading and gets in front of it. That instinct is what helps invent the next format instead of only shipping the current one.

▶ See PROPEL ⓘ How he reads the market
↗ AI film is going festival-grade. Runway's AIFF and Project Odyssey are minting a real creator class — entertainment-quality work on a brand-new supply chain.
⟳ The tooling churns fast. Model and API sunsets keep forcing creators to rebuild pipelines — operators who can absorb that change win.
◈ The missing layer is trust. No standard way to co-create and split credit — the exact gap PROPEL's NDA-first collaboration and ContribLog attribution close.
